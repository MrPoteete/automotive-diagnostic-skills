# Comprehensive Automotive Warranty Failures Reference Guide
## US Vehicles 2005-Present | For ASE Master Technicians

This comprehensive diagnostic reference compiles documented, verifiable common component failures across all major automotive brands based exclusively on NHTSA databases, manufacturer Technical Service Bulletins, official recall databases, extended warranty programs, verified class action settlements, and reputable automotive technical sources.

## FORD MOTOR COMPANY

### 1. Door Latch Failures
- **Component:** Side door latch pawl spring tab
- **Vehicles:** 2011-2016 Fiesta, Focus, Fusion, Escape, C-MAX, Transit Connect, Mustang, MKZ, MKC (2.3M+ vehicles)
- **Failure:** Pawl spring tab breaks due to extreme temperatures preventing door latching or causing doors to open while driving
- **Source:** https://www.nhtsa.gov/recall-spotlight/ford
- **NHTSA:** 16V643, 20V331 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

### 2. PowerShift Dual-Clutch Transmission
- **Component:** DPS6 PowerShift transmission
- **Vehicles:** 2011-2019 Fiesta, 2012-2018 Focus
- **Failure:** "Dry" clutch overheats causing slipping, bucking, jerking, loss of propulsion, catastrophic failure. No permanent fix exists per Ford.
- **Source:** https://fordtransmissionsettlement.com/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - $77M class action settlement

### 3. EcoBoost Engine Failures

**3A. 1.5L - Cracked Fuel Injectors**
- **Vehicles:** 2020-2022 Bronco Sport, Escape (521,778 vehicles)
- **Failure:** Cracked injectors leak up to 5 gal/hr, fire hazard, 54 engine fires
- **Source:** https://carbuzz.com/the-largest-ford-ecoboost-recalls-in-15-years-of-production/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

**3B. 2.7L & 3.0L - Intake Valve Failures**
- **Vehicles:** 2021-2022 F-150, Bronco, Explorer, Edge, Aviator, Nautilus (90,736 vehicles)
- **Failure:** Intake valves crack/break causing catastrophic engine failure, complete loss of power while driving
- **Source:** https://www.injuryrelief.com/blog/ford-ecoboost-recall-engine-failure-defect/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

**3C. 1.0L - Oil Pump Failures**
- **Vehicles:** 2013-2021 Focus, Fiesta, EcoSport (139,739 vehicles)
- **Failure:** Oil pump fracture causing complete engine failure requiring replacement
- **Source:** https://carbuzz.com/the-largest-ford-ecoboost-recalls-in-15-years-of-production/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

**3D. 1.5L/1.6L/2.0L - Coolant Leaks**
- **Vehicles:** 2013-2019 Escape, Fusion, Edge, MKC, MKZ
- **Failure:** Internal coolant leaks into cylinders causing overheating, engine damage, engine fires
- **Source:** https://lemonlawexperts.com/ford-coolant-leak-recall/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Class action lawsuit, documented fires

**3E. 3.5L - Multiple Issues**
- **Vehicles:** 2011-2025 F-150, Explorer, Expedition, Navigator
- **Failure:** Misaligned oil plugs (fire risk), exhaust manifold warping, turbocharger failures, intercooler condensation
- **Source:** https://fordauthority.com/2024/11/2024-2025-ford-3-5l-v6-ecoboost-models-recalled-over-oil-leak-issue/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

### 4. Cam Phaser Failures
- **Component:** Variable valve timing cam phasers
- **Vehicles:** 2017-2020 F-150, Expedition, Navigator (3.5L EcoBoost)
- **Failure:** Cam phasers produce loud rattling on cold start, can cause engine damage. Customer Satisfaction Program (not full recall) with prorated cost. Repairs $2,700-$4,400.
- **Source:** https://www.mylemon.com/lemon-law-blog/ford-cam-phaser-problems-what-you-need-to-do-now/
- **TSB:** 21N03 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

### 5. 10-Speed Transmission Problems
- **Component:** 10R80/10R60 automatic transmission
- **Vehicles:** 2017-2024 F-150, Ranger, Bronco, Explorer, Mustang, Expedition, Aviator
- **Failure:** Hard shifting, jerking, loss of power, false low pressure warnings causing unexpected neutral shift, parking pawl failure (rollaway), reverse gear failure. 3,400+ NHTSA complaints.
- **Source:** https://lemonlawfirm.com/ford-10-speed-transmission-problems/
- **NHTSA:** 22V-188, 23V-070, 25V-315 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

### 6. Spark Plug Breakage/Ejection (5.4L Triton)
- **Component:** Spark plugs in 5.4L 3-valve engines
- **Vehicles:** 2004-2008 F-150, Mustang, Explorer, Expedition, Navigator, Mark LT
- **Failure:** Two-piece spark plugs break during removal ($800-$1,200 repair). Separate issue: plugs eject from heads due to inadequate threads (fire risk).
- **Source:** https://topclassactions.com/lawsuit-settlements/closed-settlements/ford-lincoln-spark-plug-class-action-settlement/
- **TSB:** 07-21-2 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - $2.2M settlement

### 7. SYNC/Rearview Camera Failures
- **Component:** SYNC infotainment APIM module
- **Vehicles:** 2020-2024 Bronco, F-150, Edge, Escape, Expedition, Explorer, Ranger, Mustang, Mach-E, Transit, Lincoln models (1.5M+ vehicles)
- **Failure:** SYNC software causes rearview camera to freeze, delay, show black screen, or fail when in reverse
- **Source:** https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/ford-consent-order-165-million-civil-penalty
- **NHTSA:** 25V315, 25V442, 25V544 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Ford paid $165M penalty

### 8. Takata Airbag Inflators
- **Component:** Takata airbag inflators
- **Vehicles:** 2004-2014 Ford/Lincoln/Mercury models (457,000+ with "Do Not Drive" warnings)
- **Failure:** Defective inflators explode propelling metal shrapnel. 27 deaths, 400+ injuries in US.
- **Source:** https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/ford-mazda-do-not-drive-warnings-takata-air-bags
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

### 9. Engine Block Heater Fires
- **Component:** Engine block heater
- **Vehicles:** 2016-2024 Explorer, Ranger, Fusion, Bronco, Bronco Sport, Maverick, Escape, Corsair, MKC (59,006 vehicles)
- **Failure:** Block heater cracks at solder joints leaking coolant into electrical components. Short circuit when plugged in creates fire hazard. 46 fires reported.
- **Source:** https://www.kare11.com/article/news/nation-world/ford-recall-engine-block-heater-fire-hazard/507-2d60b622-44c3-47c2-a8d5-8d5d51ffc54e
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

## GENERAL MOTORS (CHEVROLET, GMC, BUICK, CADILLAC)

### 1. L87 6.2L V8 Catastrophic Engine Failure
- **Component:** Connecting rod and crankshaft bearings
- **Vehicles:** 2021-2024 Silverado, Sierra, Tahoe, Suburban, Yukon, Escalade (597,630 vehicles)
- **Failure:** Catastrophic engine failure with NO WARNING. Sediment on connecting rods and out-of-spec crankshaft causes bearing damage, engine seizure, or connecting rod breaching block. 28,102 complaints, 14,332 loss of propulsion cases.
- **Source:** https://static.nhtsa.gov/odi/rcl/2025/RCLRPT-25V274-1598.PDF
- **NHTSA:** 25V-274 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

### 2. Active Fuel Management (AFM/DFM) Lifter Failures
- **Component:** AFM/DFM valve lifters and valve train
- **Vehicles:** 2007-2024+ vehicles with 5.3L, 6.0L, 6.2L V8 (Silverado, Sierra, Tahoe, Suburban, Yukon, Escalade, Traverse, Enclave, Acadia)
- **Failure:** AFM lifters collapse or fail causing misfires (P0300-P0308), ticking, bent pushrods, camshaft damage, complete engine failure. Excessive oil consumption (1 qt per 1,000 mi).
- **Source:** https://www.gmlifterlawsuit.com/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Active class action, 60+ TSBs

### 3. 5.3L Vortec LC9 - Piston/Oil Consumption
- **Component:** Piston assembly and piston rings
- **Vehicles:** 2011-2014 Avalanche, Silverado, Suburban, Tahoe, Sierra, Yukon (manufactured after 2/10/2011)
- **Failure:** Defective pistons cause excessive oil consumption, spark plug fouling, rough running, permanent engine damage
- **Source:** https://thedetroitbureau.com/2022/10/gm-hit-with-102-6-million-judgment-in-engine-case/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - $102.6M settlement (38,000 plaintiffs @ $2,700 each)

### 4. 10-Speed Transmission Rear Wheel Lock-Up
- **Component:** 10L80/10L90 transmission control valve
- **Vehicles:** 2020-2022 Silverado, Sierra, Tahoe, Suburban, Yukon, Escalade diesel (461,839 vehicles)
- **Failure:** Control valve wear causes harsh shifting, momentary rear wheel lock-up during downshift from 8th gear. Can cause vehicle to veer off road. 1,888 field reports.
- **Source:** https://www.automotivedive.com/news/gm-recalls-462k-trucks-suv-tranmisssion-rear-wheel-lockup-nhtsa/732952/
- **NHTSA:** 24V-797 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

### 5. 10-Speed Transmission - Passenger Cars
- **Component:** Transmission control valve
- **Vehicles:** 2019-2022 Cadillac CT6, CT4, CT5; 2020-2022 Camaro (90,081 vehicles)
- **Failure:** Control valves wear causing pressure loss, harsh shifting, momentary wheel lock-up (<150ms). 115 lock-up reports, 1 confirmed accident.
- **Source:** https://www.nbcnews.com/business/recall/general-motors-vehicle-recalled-transmission-problem-wheels-lock-rcna196646
- **NHTSA:** 25V-148 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

### 6. 8-Speed Automatic Transmission Issues
- **Component:** 8L45/8L90 torque converter and TCM
- **Vehicles:** 2015-2023+ Cadillac, Chevrolet, GMC (800,000+ vehicles)
- **Failure:** Harsh engagement, sudden acceleration, stumbling, shuddering, slipping/jerking. 60+ TSBs, NO recall.
- **Source:** https://gmauthority.com/blog/2025/03/gm-fails-to-get-8-speed-automatic-transmission-class-action-lawsuit-dismissed/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Federal court class action certified

### 7. Ignition Switch Recall (Historic)
- **Component:** Ignition switch torque performance
- **Vehicles:** 2003-2014 (29M vehicles cumulative) - Primary: 2005-2007 Cobalt, 2007 G5, 2003-2007 Ion
- **Failure:** Switch moves out of "RUN" causing engine shutdown while driving, disabling power steering and airbags. 124 deaths, 275+ injuries.
- **Source:** https://www.transportation.gov/testimony/gm-ignition-switch-recall-why-did-it-take-so-long
- **NHTSA:** 14V-047, 14V-152, 14V-346, 14V-355, 14V-400
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - $35M penalty, $900M deferred prosecution, Congressional hearings

### 8. Takata Airbag Inflators
- **Component:** Front passenger airbag inflator
- **Vehicles:** 2007-2014 Escalade, Silverado, Suburban, Tahoe, Sierra, Yukon (5.9M vehicles)
- **Failure:** Propellant degrades causing explosive rupture with metal fragments. 18 deaths nationwide.
- **Source:** https://www.gmtakataairbag.com/
- **NHTSA:** 21V-050 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

### 9. ARC Automotive Airbag Inflators
- **Component:** Driver front airbag inflator
- **Vehicles:** 2014-2017 Enclave, Traverse, Acadia (995,000 vehicles)
- **Failure:** Driver airbag may explode during deployment. 2 deaths, 7 injuries, 3 confirmed ruptures.
- **Source:** https://www.carpro.com/blog/gm-recalls-nearly-1-million-vehicles-over-defective-air-bags
- **NHTSA:** 21V-782, 22V-246, 23V-342 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

### 10. HVAC/Air Conditioning Failures
- **Component:** AC condenser, compressor-to-condenser line
- **Vehicles:** 2014-2017 Escalade, Silverado 1500, Suburban, Tahoe, Sierra 1500, Yukon; 2018 Enclave, XT5, Traverse, Acadia
- **Failure:** AC condensers crack, compressor-to-condenser line separates causing refrigerant leaks. Repair $1,600-$3,600.
- **Source:** https://topclassactions.com/lawsuit-settlements/lawsuit-news/general-motors-class-action-lawsuit-and-settlement-news/gm-cant-shake-faulty-air-conditioning-class-action-lawsuit-judge-rules/
- **TSBs:** PIT5331, PIE0340 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Federal class action ongoing

## STELLANTIS (DODGE, RAM, CHRYSLER, JEEP)

### 1. TIPM (Totally Integrated Power Module) Failures
- **Component:** TIPM fuel pump relay and power distribution
- **Vehicles:** 2005-2016 Journey, Grand Caravan, Town & Country, Wrangler, Grand Cherokee, Patriot, Compass, Durango, 300, Charger, Challenger (7M vehicles estimated)
- **Failure:** Fuel pump relay failure causing no-start or continuous pump operation. Random electrical failures. Multiple TIPM designs (4+ variants per year) complicate diagnosis.
- **Source:** http://www.chryslerproblems.com/tipm/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Class action settlement 2016

### 2. 68RFE Transmission Failures
- **Component:** 68RFE 6-speed automatic transmission
- **Vehicles:** 2007.5-2019 Ram 2500/3500 (6.7L Cummins and gas); 2008-2012 Grand Cherokee diesel
- **Failure:** Valve body cross-leaks (pre-2011 non-anodized aluminum), torque converter failure, clutch burning (P0733, P1DA8), oil pump leaks, loss of 3rd/6th gear, overheating (P0871).
- **Source:** https://nextgendiesel.com/blogs/transmissions-101/transmissions-101-the-dodge-68rfe-problems-solutions-upgrades
- **TSB:** 21-010-12 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

### 3. Pentastar 3.6L V6 Multiple Failures
- **Component:** Various 3.6L Pentastar V6 components
- **Vehicles:** 2011-2020 Wrangler, Grand Cherokee, Cherokee, Charger, Challenger, Durango, Journey, 200, 300, Town & Country, Pacifica, Ram

**A. Left Cylinder Head Cracks (2011-2013)**
- Cylinder #2 valve seat overheating causing head cracks, misfires (P0302), ticking, stalling
- Extended Warranty: 10 years/150,000 miles (2011-2013 only)

**B. Rocker Arm/Cam Follower Failure**
- Roller bearing failure causing persistent ticking, misfires, rough idle, metal debris contaminating oil
- TSB March 2014 issued revised rockers but not permanent fix

**C. Oil Cooler/Filter Housing Failure**
- Plastic housing cracks (especially 2014+ around 50K miles) causing oil leak into engine valley (fire risk)

- **Source:** https://www.motorreviewer.com/engine.php?engine_id=167
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Multiple TSBs, class action lawsuit

### 4. HEMI 5.7L Cam/Lifter Failures
- **Component:** 5.7L HEMI V8 camshaft and lifter assembly
- **Vehicles:** 2009+ Ram 1500/2500/3500, Grand Cherokee, Durango, Charger, Challenger, 300 (VVT-equipped, most problematic 2009-2016)
- **Failure:** Roller lifter needle bearing failure, camshaft lobe wear/grinding, engine ticking, misfires, metal shavings, complete engine failure. ~5% failure rate. Repair $6,000+.
- **Source:** https://www.ramforumz.com/threads/5-7-vvt-cam-lifter-failures-the-problem-and-the-solution.270029/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - No official recall, considered "maintenance item"

### 5. 3.0L EcoDiesel V6 Multiple Failures
- **Component:** 3.0L EcoDiesel V6 (VM Motori)
- **Vehicles:** 2014-2023 Ram 1500, Grand Cherokee, Gladiator, Wrangler

**A. EGR Cooler Cracks** (2014-2016 Critical)
- EGR cooler cracks causing coolant leaks, overheating, fire risk
- RECALL: 2014-2016 models

**B. Oil Cooler Failure**
- Plastic check valve failure causing oil to mix with coolant, low oil pressure, complete engine damage

**C. CP4.2 Fuel Pump Failure**
- High-pressure fuel pump self-destructs contaminating entire fuel system
- RECALL: June 2022

- **Source:** https://www.motorreviewer.com/engine.php?engine_id=191
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - EPA settlement, 35-40% major component failure after 100K miles

### 6. Takata Airbag Recall
- **Component:** Takata airbag inflators
- **Vehicles:** 2003-2016 various models including Ram, Durango, 300, Charger, Wrangler, Challenger
- **STOP-DRIVE ADVISORY:** 2003 Ram, 2005-2010 300/Charger/Magnum/Challenger
- **Failure:** Ammonium nitrate degradation causing inflator rupture and metal shrapnel. 27 deaths nationwide.
- **Source:** https://www.nhtsa.gov/vehicle-safety/takata-recall-spotlight
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - ONGOING

### 7. Jeep Fuel Tank Fire Hazard
- **Component:** Rear-mounted plastic fuel tank
- **Vehicles:** 1993-2004 Grand Cherokee, 2002-2007 Liberty
- **Failure:** Fuel tank behind rear axle ruptures in rear-end collisions causing fires/explosions. 75-270 fire-related deaths. 400% higher fire risk than comparable SUVs.
- **Source:** https://www.autosafety.org/major-recalls-jeep-grand-cherokee-fuel-tank/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - NHTSA investigation EA12-005, partial voluntary recall (1.56M vehicles)

## TOYOTA

### 1. Frame Rust/Corrosion
- **Component:** Frame/chassis rust-through perforation
- **Vehicles:** 2005-2010 Tacoma (all trims), 2005-2008 Sequoia, 2007-2008 Tundra, 2005-2011 4Runner
- **Failure:** Excessive frame rust from inadequate rust-proofing (Dana-manufactured). Severe in rear section near catalytic converter. Can cause leaf spring separation, fuel tank damage, brake line damage, catastrophic frame failure. Most prevalent in salt-belt states.
- **Source:** https://www.wcpo.com/money/consumer/dont-waste-your-money/warranty-running-out-for-rusting-toyota-frames
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - $3.4B class action settlement (2016), coverage 12-15 years

### 2. Takata Airbag Inflators
- **Component:** Takata airbag inflators
- **Vehicles:** 2001-2019 multiple Toyota/Lexus models
- **Failure:** Inflators rupture explosively sending metal shrapnel into cabin. 35+ deaths worldwide, 400+ injuries in US.
- **Source:** https://www.nhtsa.gov/vehicle-safety/takata-recall-spotlight
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - $741M class action settlements

### 3. ZF-TRW Airbag Control Units
- **Component:** ZF-TRW airbag control units with ASIC component
- **Vehicles:** 2011-2019 Corolla, Matrix, Avalon/Avalon Hybrid, Tacoma, Tundra, Sequoia
- **Failure:** ACUs vulnerable to electrical overstress causing airbags/safety systems to fail to deploy during collision.
- **Source:** https://www.airbagcontrolunitsettlement.com/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - $78.5M settlement (2023), 15-year extended warranty

### 4. Denso Fuel Pump Failure
- **Component:** Denso low-pressure fuel pump (plastic impeller)
- **Vehicles:** 2013-2020 Toyota/Lexus (4Runner, Avalon, Camry, Highlander, Land Cruiser, Sequoia, Corolla, Tacoma, Sienna, Tundra, multiple Lexus models)
- **Failure:** Fuel pump impeller unstable causing pump failure without warning. Engine stalling, loss of power, crash risk.
- **Source:** https://www.toyotafuelpumpssettlement.com/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - $150M settlement, 15-year extended warranty, 5.8M vehicles recalled

### 5. Tundra/Lexus LX V35A Engine Debris
- **Component:** 3.4L V35A twin-turbo V6 engine
- **Vehicles:** 2022-2024 Tundra/Tundra Hybrid, 2022-2023 Lexus LX600
- **Failure:** Manufacturing debris left inside engine causing engine knocking, rough running, stalling, catastrophic engine failure. Complete engine replacement required.
- **Source:** https://topclassactions.com/toyota-class-action-lawsuit-and-settlement-news/toyota-class-action-claims-some-tundras-have-engine-defect/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Recall May 2024 (102,000+ vehicles), class action lawsuit Dec 2024

### 6. 2024 Tacoma Transmission Failures
- **Component:** 8-speed automatic transmission and torque converter
- **Vehicles:** 2024 Toyota Tacoma
- **Failure:** Sudden shift into neutral, stuck in high gears, delayed/harsh engagement, complete transmission failure at very low mileage. Metal shavings in fluid.
- **Source:** https://www.thedrive.com/news/2024-toyota-tacoma-owners-keep-reporting-transmission-failures
- **MEDIUM-HIGH CONFIDENCE** - TSB issued, class action lawsuit filed, 13+ NHTSA complaints

### 7. RAV4 Battery Terminal Assembly Failure
- **Component:** Battery terminal/battery assembly
- **Vehicles:** 2013-2018 Toyota RAV4 (non-hybrid)
- **Failure:** Battery terminals vulnerable to failure causing loss of electrical power, vehicle stalling, potential engine compartment fire risk.
- **Source:** https://rav4batteryassemblysettlement.com/
- **NHTSA:** 23V-734 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Settlement approved Nov 2024

### 8. Engine Block Casting Defects
- **Component:** 2.5L 4-cylinder engine block
- **Vehicles:** 2020 Camry/Camry Hybrid, 2020 Avalon Hybrid, 2019-2020 RAV4/RAV4 Hybrid, 2020 Lexus ES300h
- **Failure:** Porosity in engine casting causes cracks, coolant leaks, overheating, engine stall, oil leaks, fire risk.
- **Source:** https://www.consumerreports.org/car-recalls-defects/toyota-lexus-recall-replace-engine-avalon-camry-rav4-es/
- **NHTSA:** 20V064 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Recall April 2020 (44,191 vehicles), complete engine replacement

## HONDA/ACURA

### 1. Takata Airbag Inflators
- **Component:** Takata airbag inflators (Alpha and PSAN types)
- **Vehicles:** 2001-2016 Honda/Acura models
- **Failure:** Explosive airbag rupture with metal fragments. Alpha inflators have 50% rupture rate. 23+ confirmed US fatalities, 28 globally. Honda accounts for 20M of 67M recalled airbags.
- **Source:** https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/consumer-alert-honda-upgrades-takata-alpha-recall-do-not-drive-warning-targeting
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Active "Do Not Drive" warnings for 2001-2003 Alpha models, $605M settlement

### 2. Excessive Oil Consumption (VCM-Related)
- **Component:** Piston rings and Variable Cylinder Management system
- **Vehicles:** 2008-2013 Accord V6, Odyssey, Pilot; 2010-2011 CR-V, Accord Crosstour
- **Failure:** VCM causes piston rings to stick causing excessive oil consumption (1 qt per 1,000 mi), spark plug fouling, misfires (P0301-P0304), potential engine damage.
- **Source:** https://www.vehiclehistory.com/articles/honda-pilot-excessive-oil-consumption-recalls
- **TSBs:** 12-089, 13-080, 16-101, 19-006
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Class action settlement 2013, 1.6M vehicles, 8-year warranty extension

### 3. VCM Engine Problems
- **Component:** VCM cylinder deactivation system
- **Vehicles:** 2005-2017 Odyssey, 2008-2017 Accord V6, 2009-2017 Pilot
- **Failure:** Cylinder deactivation causes excessive oil consumption, fouled spark plugs, misfires, premature engine mount failure ($800+), transmission torque converter damage, catalytic converter failure (P0420/P3400/P3497), vibration/shudder.
- **Source:** https://www.vcmtuner.com/pages/honda-acura-lawsuit
- **TSB:** A11-033 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Multiple TSBs, warranty extensions

### 4. 9-Speed Automatic Transmission Defect
- **Component:** ZF 9HP 9-speed automatic transmission
- **Vehicles:** 2016-2022 Pilot, 2018-2019 Odyssey, 2019-2023 Passport, 2020-2023 Ridgeline
- **Failure:** Programming defect causes rough/delayed shifting, harsh gear engagement, loud noises, sudden harsh accelerations/decelerations, loss of power, juddering.
- **Source:** https://www.hondatransmissionlawsuit.com/
- **MEDIUM-HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Active class action lawsuit, multiple TSBs but no permanent fix

### 5. Odyssey Transmission Failures
- **Component:** 5-speed/6-speed automatic transmission
- **Vehicles:** 2001-2004 Odyssey (5-speed), 2012-2016 Odyssey (6-speed)
- **Failure:** Third clutch causes sudden downshift from 5th to 2nd at highway speeds. 6-speed models experience shudder, judder, vibration.
- **Source:** https://topclassactions.com/lawsuit-settlements/closed-settlements/honda-5-speed-automatic-transmission-class-action-settlement/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - $2M settlement 2021 for 5-speed CA owners

### 6. Infotainment System Failures
- **Component:** Infotainment system display unit
- **Vehicles:** 2018-2019 Odyssey, 2019 Pilot, 2019 Passport
- **Failure:** Infotainment fails to boot, freezes during use, malfunctions. Issues appearing with as few as 580 miles.
- **Source:** https://www.hbsslaw.com/cases/honda-infotainment-issue-class-action-lawsuit
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - $33M settlement (2022), ~450,000 owners

## NISSAN

### 1. CVT Transmission Failures (WIDESPREAD)
- **Component:** Continuously Variable Transmission - Jatco RE0F10A, RE0F11A
- **Vehicles:** 2013-2024 Altima, Sentra, Rogue, Murano, Pathfinder, Versa, Juke
- **Failure:** Shuddering, jerking, hesitation, complete loss of power/sudden failure, overheating, belt and pulley wear, control valve failures. Average failure 53,000-70,000 miles.
- **Source:** https://static.nhtsa.gov/odi/tsbs/2023/MC-10246457-0001.pdf
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Multiple settlements ($277M+), 10,000+ NHTSA complaints, warranty extended to 84 months/84,000 miles

### 2. Radiator/Transmission Cooler Failure ("Strawberry Milkshake of Death")
- **Component:** Internal transmission oil cooler in radiator
- **Vehicles:** 2005-2010 Pathfinder, Frontier, Xterra (automatic transmission)
- **Failure:** Radiator cooler tube cracks internally mixing coolant with transmission fluid creating pink "milky" contaminated fluid. Results in catastrophic transmission failure. Average failure 90,000-100,000 miles. Repair $3,500-$6,000+.
- **Source:** http://www.nissanproblems.com/coolant-leaks-destroy-transmission/
- **NHTSA:** 10V517000 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Class action settlement 2013, warranty extension to 8 years/80,000 miles

## SUBARU

### 1. Head Gasket Failures
- **Component:** Cylinder head gaskets on 2.5L SOHC engines
- **Vehicles:** 1996-2011 Impreza, Forester, Legacy, Outback, Baja (2.5L SOHC non-turbo). Primary issue years: 1999-2004, 2006-2011.
- **Failure:** External oil/coolant leaks from head gasket, overheating, coolant loss, white exhaust smoke. Typical failure 60,000-100,000 miles. Repair $2,200-$2,500 (includes timing belt, water pump, thermostats).
- **Source:** https://www.smart-service.com/blog/subaru-warranty/subaru-head-gasket-will-subaru-pay-for-repair/
- **HIGH CONFIDENCE** - Class action settled 2016 with warranty extension

### 2. Wheel Bearing/Hub Assembly Premature Failure
- **Component:** Rear wheel hub and bearing assemblies, backing plates
- **Vehicles:** 2012-2019 Impreza/Crosstrek, 2014-2019 Forester, Outback, 2015-2019 WRX, 2018-2021 Legacy
- **Failure:** Premature rear wheel bearing failure (often both sides). Typical failure 40,000-70,000 miles (should last 100,000+). Howling/whirring noise increases with speed. Caused by defective backing plate design.
- **Source:** https://static.nhtsa.gov/odi/tsbs/2020/MC-10185064-0001.pdf
- **TSBs:** 05-70-19R, 05-84-21 | **HIGH CONFIDENCE**

### 3. Excessive Oil Consumption
- **Component:** Piston rings, cylinder walls (FB20 2.0L and FB25 2.5L engines)
- **Vehicles:** 2011-2015 Forester, Legacy, Outback, Impreza, XV Crosstrek (specific VIN ranges)

## KEY DIAGNOSTIC NOTES FOR TECHNICIANS

**FORD:**
- EcoBoost engines: Multiple failure modes by displacement. Check for coolant leaks, oil leaks, intercooler condensation TSB.
- 10-speed transmission: Check for software updates before condemning. Document harsh shifts thoroughly.
- Cam phasers (3.5L EcoBoost): Listen for cold start rattle. Customer Satisfaction Program has time limits.

**GM:**
- L87 6.2L: Check for unusual noises immediately. Catastrophic failure with little warning.
- AFM/DFM: Check for ticking, misfires P0300-P0308, excessive oil consumption. Disabling AFM may prevent further damage.
- Ignition switch (2003-2014): Check for key weight, verify repair status. Critical safety issue.

**STELLANTIS:**
- TIPM: Multiple variants per model year. Check for metal shavings in oil filter, inspect VVT solenoid screen. Third-party rebuilds available $200-350 vs $1,200 dealer replacement.
- 68RFE: Pre-2011 valve bodies should be replaced before 100K miles. Install revised backing plates or failure will recur.
- HEMI 5.7L cam/lifters: 5,000-mile oil change intervals MAXIMUM. Monitor for ticking - early intervention critical.

**TOYOTA:**
- Frame rust (2005-2010): Most severe in salt-belt states. Check rear section near catalytic converter and leaf spring mounts.
- V35A engine debris (2022-2024 Tundra): Complete engine replacement may be required.

**HONDA:**
- VCM oil consumption: Perform proper oil consumption test (1,200 mi test, >1/3 qt = failure). Extended warranty may still apply.
- Takata Alpha inflators (2001-2003): "Do Not Drive" warning still active. 50% rupture rate.

**NISSAN:**
- CVT: Check VIN against warranty extension programs before quoting. Document all symptoms thoroughly. Average failure 53K-70K miles.
- Radiator/trans cooler (2005-2010): Inspect coolant reservoir for "strawberry milkshake" appearance. Recommend external trans cooler bypass as preventive measure.

**SUBARU:**
- Head gaskets: External leak inspection at every service on 1996-2011 2.5L SOHC engines. Plan for timing belt, water pump, thermostats when performing head gasket work.
- Wheel bearings: MUST install revised backing plates per TSB or failure will recur. Part numbers specified in TSB 05-70-19R and 05-84-21.

**MAZDA:**
- SKYACTIV 2.5T coolant leak: Inspect cylinder head around exhaust manifold. Contact Mazda North America Operations for goodwill assistance - many receiving substantial coverage out of warranty.
- Carbon buildup (DI engines): Recommend walnut blasting intake valve cleaning at 80K-100K miles.

**HYUNDAI/KIA:**
- Theta II engines: Check for metal shavings in oil, unusual noises. Lifetime warranty extension available for some VINs.
- ABS module fire risk: "Park Outside" advisory still in effect. 21 vehicle fires + 22 thermal incidents reported.
- Theft vulnerability (2011-2022): Software update available for some; steering wheel locks for incompatible vehicles.

**VW/AUDI:**
- EA888 2.0T timing chain: Listen for timing chain rattle on cold start. Failures common 40K-80K miles despite 120K maintenance intervals.
- DSG: Check shift quality, mechatronic adaptation values. Multiple TSBs and recalls.

**BMW:**
- N20/N26 timing chain: Check timing chain stretch with special tools, listen for rattle. Widespread failures 50K-90K miles.
- N52 VANOS bolts: Inspect VANOS bolt condition, check for oil leaks at VANOS units. Expanded recall 2023.

**MERCEDES:**
- Airmatic: Check for air leaks, compressor operation, uneven ride height. Repair costs $1,500-$4,000 per corner.
- HVAC mold: Check for musty odors. Class action settlement provides up to $900 reimbursement.
- Brake booster (2006-2012 ML/GL/R): Inspect for corrosion. "Do Not Drive" warning issued for some vehicles.

**VOLVO:**
- Fuel pump (2019-2020): Verify recall completion. Fails while vehicle in motion causing sudden stall.
- PHEV battery (2020-2022): "Do Not Charge" warning issued May 2025. Fire risk when fully charged and parked.
- Rearview camera (2021-2025): Software error. Some vehicles also have brake control module failures after update.

## IMPORTANT DISCLAIMERS

1. **VIN Verification Required:** Always verify specific VIN eligibility using NHTSA.gov VIN lookup tool or manufacturer systems before advising customers on recall/warranty coverage.

2. **Warranty Extensions:** Many issues received extended warranty coverage but with time/mileage limits that have now expired for many vehicles. Some manufacturers use "Customer Satisfaction Programs" requiring customer cost-sharing rather than full recalls.

3. **Repeated Recalls:** Several issues (Ford door latches, rearview cameras; GM transmissions) required multiple recall attempts as initial fixes proved inadequate.

4. **Class Action Limitations:** Class action settlements typically provide partial reimbursement only and have strict claim filing deadlines. Many settlements are now closed to new claimants.

5. **Documentation Critical:** Document all failures thoroughly for potential goodwill claims and class action participation. Maintenance history often required for warranty claims.

6. **"Secret Warranties":** Many widespread defects have service campaigns or warranty extensions not publicized. Check NHTSA manufacturer communications tab for non-recall service campaigns.
