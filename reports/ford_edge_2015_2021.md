## Executive Summary
The 2015–2021 Ford Edge carries a 4,411-complaint record dominated by engine and powertrain failures — particularly coolant intrusion into the 2.0L EcoBoost, torque converter failures, and transmission shift complaints that together account for over 50% of total volume. Complaint volume peaked sharply in 2017 at 1,205 and has not fully normalized, indicating persistent mechanical issues rather than reporting lag alone. **24 NHTSA recalls are open for this model range** — covering brakes, steering, transmission, airbags, and seat belts — and recall status must be confirmed on every unit before diagnosis begins. With 71 TSBs on file and multiple active powertrain software fixes, shops should pull TSBs and verify recall coverage before touching hardware.

## Failure Pattern Analysis

Derived from keyword analysis of complaint narratives for the top failure system.

### Check Engine / MIL Illumination
**116 complaints mention this pattern (58.0%)**

This is the most common presenting symptom across the Edge complaint record and is almost never a standalone issue on this platform. In the majority of cases, the MIL is secondary to coolant intrusion into the 2.0L EcoBoost, rough idle, or early misfire activity. Don't clear and send — the MIL on these units is frequently the first visible signal of a developing long-block problem. Pull all stored and pending codes, cross-reference against the TSB library before any parts are ordered, and inspect coolant level and condition on the same visit.

> *"At that point, the check engine light illuminated"*
> *"When I parked the check engine light came on"*
> *"The contact stated that the vehicle was idling roughly, and the check engine warning light was illuminated"*

### HVAC / AC — Coolant Intrusion
**74 complaints mention this pattern (37.0%)**

Despite the category label, the dominant complaint in this cluster is not HVAC — it's coolant intrusion into the 2.0L EcoBoost engine, a documented and recurring failure mode on 2015–2018 units. Customers typically report unexplained coolant loss, sputtering or vibration at idle, and a history of "top-off" visits before the real problem surfaces. Ford issued TSBs and performed long-block replacements under warranty on affected units. On any 2.0L EcoBoost with unexplained coolant loss, rough idle, or white exhaust at startup: pull coolant condition, check for combustion gases in the coolant, and inspect for milky residue at the oil cap before committing to a minor repair.

> *"0L EcoBoost engine and coolant intrusion"*
> *"Long block repair due to coolant intrusion which is a known issue with this ford edge model"*
> *"The coolant level would drop/leak randomly causing car to sputter or vibrate while sitting at traffic lights"*

### Electrical / Instrument Cluster
**57 complaints mention this pattern (28.5%)**

These complaints describe intermittent warning light behavior, instrument cluster dropouts, and scenarios where warning lights either fire without cause or fail to illuminate during an active fault. The electrical system leads all TSB categories on this platform with 14 bulletins, plus 4 software-specific TSBs — a strong indicator that many of these complaints have software root causes rather than hardware failures. Before condemning sensors, modules, or wiring, check for applicable PCM/cluster software updates. Intermittent faults with no stored codes are a red flag for software or ground issues on this generation.

### Stalling / Loss of Power
**49 complaints mention this pattern (24.5%)**

Sudden stall and loss of power complaints on the Edge span multiple root causes, but the most severe involve fuel system contamination and engine damage on 2.0L EcoBoost units — including at least one complaint referencing a diesel smell in the exhaust and an engine that would not restart. Also represented here: long-block failures tied to the coolant intrusion issue and cases where dealers failed to install corrected replacement blocks. On any stall/no-restart presentation, confirm the replacement engine part number against the current TSB before clearing the job. An incorrect block installed under a prior repair is a documented repeat-failure scenario.

> *"The exhaust smelled like diesel fuel and that if the engine were shut off, it was highly unlikely to restart"*
> *"The contact stated while driving at an undisclosed speed, the contact stated that the vehicle stalled"*
> *"DEALER FAILED TO INSTALL CORRECTED OR UPDATED BLOCK"*

### Engine Misfires / Rough Running
**49 complaints mention this pattern (24.5%)**

Misfire complaints on the Edge are heavily concentrated in 2.0L EcoBoost units and are frequently traced back to coolant intrusion fouling cylinders or degrading plugs and coils. Rough idle at a stop, vibration under light throttle, and a flashing MIL under load are the typical presentation. P030X misfire codes — particularly P0301 — appear repeatedly in complaint narratives. Do not treat these as routine tune-up items without ruling out internal coolant ingestion. Inspect all plugs for coolant contamination or unusual deposit patterns, and check for cylinder-specific lean conditions before assuming ignition system wear.

> *"The coolant level would drop/leak randomly causing car to sputter or vibrate while sitting at traffic lights"*
> *"The contact stated that the vehicle was idling roughly, and the check engine warning light was illuminated"*
> *"Had a misfire when starting the vehicle so took it to a mechanic"*

### Steering
**32 complaints mention this pattern (16.0%)**

Steering complaints on this platform are backed by two separate NHTSA recalls covering the Electric Power Assist System (EPAS) — campaigns 19V632000 and 15V250000 — affecting 2015–2016 model years. Complaint narratives in this cluster also mix in misfire and stall data, suggesting some cross-contamination in complaint categorization, but the underlying steering issue is real and recall-covered. On any 2015–2016 unit with steering feel complaints, pulling effort, or EPAS warning: verify both recall campaigns are closed on the VIN before diagnosing hardware. C1277 (Steering Wheel Angle Circuit Failure) and C1443 (Phase A Short to Ground) are the high-severity steering DTCs to prioritize on this vehicle.

> *"The code P0301:00-EC was pulled from the vehicles scan"*
> *"Under normal driving conditions, engine had severe misfires, flashing check engine light, and vehicle had to be pulled to side of highway"*
> *"The contact pulled over to the side of the road, turned off, and restarted the vehicle, and smoke came from the exhaust pipe"*

### Transmission Shudder / Shift Issues
**19 complaints mention this pattern (9.5%)**

Two NHTSA recall campaigns (18V390000 and 17V427000) specifically address torque converter failures on 2017–2018 2.0L EcoBoost units, and a third (20V550000) covers automatic transmission failures on 2019–2020 models. A November 2023 TSB also addresses AWD noise, vibration, and shudder on 2019–2021 units. Customer-reported symptoms include shudder at highway speed, failure to upshift at low speed, and complete transmission replacement. On any shift complaint or driveline shudder, confirm all three recall campaigns against the VIN before road-testing for a diagnosis — recall-covered transmission work is not billable and misdiagnosing a recall-covered failure wastes significant shop time.

> *"The contact stated that while driving at approximately 5 MPH, the transmission failed to shift as needed"*
> *"The contact stated while driving 75 MPH, the vehicle was shuddering, and there was an abnormal sound coming from underneath the hood"*

### Brakes
**13 complaints mention this pattern (6.5%)**

Brake complaints on the Edge are directly tied to multiple recall campaigns: front brake hose failure (20V469000, Transport Canada 2020373), rear brake jounce hose failure (25V544000, Transport Canada 2025435), and ABS/traction control issues (16V733000). NHTSA investigation PE24027 — now closed — was opened specifically for rear brake hose failures causing progressive fluid loss and brake fade. A 2015 flexplate cracking complaint also surfaced in this cluster, indicating some narrative cross-contamination. The brake hose failure mode is the priority: fluid loss is progressive, customers may not notice until braking is seriously compromised, and the recall campaigns span 2015–2018. Any brake complaint or spongy pedal on those years — pull the VIN and check all brake-related campaigns before touching hardware.

> *"The 2015 Ford Edge and similar models are notorious for flex plate cracking, which is an absolute hazard to anyone driving it"*
> *"The contact stated that the vehicle was taken to the local dealer to be repaired under NHTSA Campaign Number: 20V469000 (Service Brakes) and 22V413000 (Power Train)"*
