## Executive Summary

The 2015–2022 Honda CR-V has generated **6,379 NHTSA complaints**, with Forward Collision Avoidance and Engine failures leading the complaint record — together accounting for over 21% of all reported issues. The 2017–2018 model years are the highest-risk vehicles in this range, producing over 3,600 complaints combined, driven largely by 1.5T powertrain problems and ADAS system faults. **17 active NHTSA recalls** are on file covering engine, fuel system, steering, airbag, and structural components — VIN verification is mandatory before any diagnosis begins on this platform.

## Failure Pattern Analysis

Derived from keyword analysis of complaint narratives for the top failure system.

### Brakes
**124 complaints mention this pattern (62.0%)**

The dominant brake complaint on this platform is **inadvertent automatic emergency braking (AEB)**, not conventional brake hardware failure. Owners report unprovoked CMBS activation at low speeds — parking lots, construction zones, highway driving — with the brake system, CMBS, and brake assist warning lights illuminating simultaneously. This is directly tied to NHTSA investigation **EA24002**, which is currently open and examining AEB software as a defect. When this vehicle comes in with brake system warning lights, do not go straight to calipers and pads. Start with the forward-facing radar/camera unit for contamination, misalignment, or calibration drift. Pull all stored CMBS and VSA codes before clearing. Confirm whether the vehicle's AEB threshold and sensitivity settings have ever been adjusted.

> *"On 8/14, As I was pulling away from a gas pump and into a parking space (going 5 mph or less), the automatic braking system alarms went off, and the brakes applied, causing the car to seize up complet"*
> *"The contact stated that while driving at 70 MPH with the cruise control activated, she noticed traffic stopped ahead and depressed the brake pedal to stop the vehicle"*
> *"Check CMBS,brake System Light& brake assist lights are on when going through construction area"*

### Electrical / Instrument Cluster
**85 complaints mention this pattern (42.5%)**

Multiple warning lights illuminating together — with no single root-cause code — is a recurring signature on this platform, particularly on 2016–2018 models. The complaint pattern suggests cascading module faults rather than isolated component failure: EPS module failures have been confirmed by dealers as a known issue on 2016–2018 CR-Vs, with steering control modules, racks, and sensors being replaced under warranty. When a customer presents with a "Christmas tree" dash, don't start replacing parts in isolation. Scan all modules for codes simultaneously, check for TSBs specific to the model year and code combination, and verify ground integrity at the engine bay and chassis ground points before ordering hardware.

> *"All warning lights came on"*
> *"No warning light was illuminated"*
> *"I was told the EPS system isn't working — the steering control module, steering rack, and all sensors replaced. Honda dealership also let me know this has been a common problem with CRV years 16, 17, 18."*

### Check Engine / MIL Light
**54 complaints mention this pattern (27.0%)**

MIL complaints span a wide mileage range — from 60,000 to 148,000 miles — indicating this is not isolated to high-wear vehicles. The 1.5T engine in 2017–2019 models has a documented history of oil dilution from gasoline intrusion, which can trigger fuel system and combustion-related codes without an obvious driveability symptom at first. When a 1.5T CR-V comes in with a MIL and no clear symptom, check oil level and condition immediately — milky, overfull, or fuel-smelling oil is a red flag. Cross-reference against active TSBs for the 1.5T before proceeding with component-level diagnosis. EPA fuel system DTCs P0232–P0234 and P1236–P1239 are on the watchlist for this platform.

> *"The failure mileage was approximately 148,000"*
> *"It's created many close calls and given me mild whiplash in the past"*

### Engine Misfires / Rough Running
**32 complaints mention this pattern (16.0%)**

Rough running and misfire complaints frequently appear alongside ADAS warning lights in the complaint narratives, which points to a broader electrical or sensor fault triggering cascading system warnings rather than a true combustion event in isolation. On the 1.5T, carbon buildup on intake valves is a known contributor to rough idle and misfire codes given the direct-injection architecture — there is no port wash on these engines. For units with mileage above 60,000 miles presenting with P030X codes and rough idle, walnut blast cleaning of intake valves should be on the table before chasing injectors or coils. Confirm oil dilution status before any engine work.

> *"My 2015 CRV suddenly had all the crash warning/adaptive cruise control/lane keep assist lights flashing…very alarming…so we brought it to White Rock Honda"*
> *"Check CMBS, brake System Light & brake assist lights are on when going through construction area"*
> *"This is a major safety issue and should be brought to attention"*

### Steering
**29 complaints mention this pattern (14.5%)**

EPS-related steering complaints break into two distinct presentations: (1) sudden hard jerk/correction at speed, and (2) steering effort increase at moderate to highway speeds. Both patterns are reflected in the TSB record — a September 2024 service bulletin specifically addresses steering effort complaints at moderate and highway speeds on this platform. Recall **18V663000** covers 2017–2018 CR-Vs for a faulty EPS magnet that can cause loss of power steering assist. On any 2017–2018 unit with a steering complaint, confirm recall completion first. If recall has been performed and the complaint persists, pull C-codes from the EPS module and check for C1277, C1278, C1441–C1443 before condemning the rack.

> *"My steering jerks hard left and corrects itself — it also accelerates while braking. Cranking the wheel turns freely and the car is very unsafe to drive — I can lose control of the vehicle."*
> *"On 8/14, As I was pulling away from a gas pump and into a parking space (going 5 mph or less), the automatic braking system alarms went off, and the brakes applied, causing the car to seize up complet"*
> *"A few days ago, pulled into a parking spot, stopped my car and placed the gear into park"*

### Stalling / Loss of Power
**12 complaints mention this pattern (6.0%)**

Loss of power and stalling complaints align with NHTSA investigation **PE25016**, currently open, examining loss of motive power across 2015–2022 CR-Vs. The investigation has received 48 Vehicle Owner Questionnaires and is a pre-recall watchlist item — treat any hard stall or loss-of-power complaint on this platform as a potential safety-critical failure. Document thoroughly. Starting hesitation is a separate but related symptom that can precede a full stall event. Check for fuel pump-related codes (P0232–P0234, P1238–P1239) and cross-reference against recall **23V858000**, which covers fuel pump delivery failures across a broad Honda model range including the CR-V.

> *"The other problem is starting — it was making a hesitation to start"*

### Transmission Shudder / Shift Issues

CVT shudder and transmission system warnings appear in the complaint record, and Honda has issued active dealer messages throughout 2024–2025 specifically targeting 2021 CR-V FHEV units with powertrain complaints. The TSB activity on Power Train is the highest of any single system in the recent TSB feed. For FHEV units, do not assume shudder is a CVT mechanical failure — Honda is actively investigating and collecting field data, which suggests a software or calibration component may be involved. Check Honda's TechLine portal for the latest dealer messages before quoting any internal transmission work on FHEV models.

### 4WD / AWD System
**3 complaints mention this pattern (1.5%)**

AWD system faults on this platform present as part of a multi-system warning event — owners report simultaneous loss of brake hold, power steering, hill start assist, AEB, and AWD warning lights, suggesting a common upstream electrical or module fault triggering downstream system shutdowns. This is the lowest-volume pattern in the record and should not be treated as an isolated AWD hardware failure without ruling out shared power, ground, or CAN bus faults first. Transport Canada recall **2024119** covers 2022 CR-Vs for EPS sensor failure that can trigger cascading system warnings — confirm Canadian units are in spec if you're servicing cross-border vehicles.

> *"Brake hold system, power steering, hill start assist, adaptive cruise control, collision mitigation, road departure, electric parking brake, TPM, emission system problem, all wheel drive system"*
